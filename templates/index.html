About Parkinson's Disease

Understanding the condition and our prediction system

Voice Analysis for Parkinson's Detection

Parkinson's disease often affects speech patterns before other more visible symptoms appear. Our system analyzes specific voice characteristics that are known to be affected by Parkinson's disease, including jitter, shimmer, and harmonic ratios.

These acoustic measurements can reveal subtle changes in voice quality that may indicate early stages of Parkinson's disease, often before clinical diagnosis is possible through traditional methods.

Our machine learning model has been trained on thousands of voice samples from both healthy individuals and Parkinson's patients to achieve high accuracy in prediction.

Key Voice Parameters We Analyze

Critical acoustic measurements for Parkinson's detection

Jitter

Measures the variation in pitch from cycle to cycle in vocal fold vibrations. Higher values may indicate instability.

Shimmer

Measures the variation in amplitude between consecutive vocal fold vibrations. Increased in Parkinson's patients.

NHR & HNR

Noise-to-Harmonics and Harmonics-to-Noise ratios measure voice quality and hoarseness.
